What is mob justice? Why is it illegal?

When a mob or a group of people become the law unto themselves and mete out justice on someone perceived as a wrongdoer. The mob acts as the victim, the jury and the executioner. It is an extrajudicial process where the people involved or the mob subvert legal processes for instant and, often violent, justice. A better word to describe such acts is vigilantism. Such acts violate a person’s right to a fair trial and the right to be innocent until proven guilty granted by both the Constitution of India and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Such acts violate freedoms granted under Article 21 (Freedom of life and liberty) and Article 14 (Equality before law) of the Constitution of India, and Article 3 (Freedom of life and liberty) and Article 10 (Right to a fair trial) under the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

Why do people lynch?

Lynching is a form of mob justice, where the perceived wrongdoer is executed. It is used as a means of establishing power and instilling terror in the minds of people or a group of people. Lynching is often a result of failure or absence of established justice, and law and order systems.

Is lynching a post-2014 phenomenon?

Lynchings in India are fairly common. What is significant is the rise in cow-related lynch mobs since 2014. Interestingly, the rise in cow-related violence is in states that have passed stringent laws against trade and slaughter of cattle since 2014.

With the increase in the cow-related lynchings, more cases of mob justice are being reported from different parts of the country, such as mobs targeting women labeled as witches in the states of West Bengal, Jharkhand, Bihar, etc. Issues that were otherwise not reported in the past are now entering the public discourse.
