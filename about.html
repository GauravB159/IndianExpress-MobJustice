About the project


What is #TheLynched?

#TheLynched aims to build a public database of mob justice related incidents in India from 2011, so that one can draw indicative insights from the data that throw more light onto the problem.

This project will operate as an imperfect work in progress and does not aim to build a precise database from which one can draw definitive facts. We are working on this now because the national conversation around lynching has taken off, however the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) does not collect data on lynchings.

Download the data

How are we defining lynching?

Human Rights Watch defines “mob justice” or “lynching” as the beating or killing of a suspected criminal by a crowd, generally consisting of civilians. reference

Building on this definition, this project is looking for incidents, where —

  • Violence or harassment is done by many on few.
  • The accused should believe that the victim has committed a crime.

How did we collect the data?

We used MIT Media Cloud to scour through IndianExpress.com from 2010 until 2017 for first reports of mob justice. This gave us a total of 100,000 plus results. Having cast a wide net, we then progressively narrowed down the result set to 250+ unique incidents.

What is new in our approach?

#TheLynched aims to go beyond "what happened" and hopes to discover correlations and indicative insights that can add to our collective understanding.

We are looking at mob justice at large and not just cow vigilantism related incidents.

Based on when and where the lynching took place, we are augmenting the data with:

  • Police to Population Ratio in that state
    • Source: Data compiled by Bureau of Police Research and Development as on Jan 2016. reference
  • Benchmarked the Police to Population Ratio against recommended ratio by United Nations.
  • Subordinate Court Judges (lower judiciary) to Population Ratio in that state.
    • Source: From Court News, publication of the Supreme Court, Vol XI Issue No. 1 Jan - Mar 2016. reference
  • Literacy of the state
  • Did the lynching take place in an urban or rural area?
  • Which political party's Chief Minister was in power?

From the story, we are trying to infer:

  • Did the local police intervene in time and prevent a possible death?
  • Prima face, was the lynching planned? We followed the following approach:
  • Religion of the victim and the accused.
  • If the allegation on the victim were true, would it be a punishable offence in that state?

Limitations of our approach

In order to provide a resource that can help contribute to ongoing national conversations about lynchings, the project has made every effort to infer the following fields for every incident in the database. These will occasionally prove inaccurate. If you know of more accurate information, please contact us as soon possible.

  1. We are analysing stories only from IndianExpress.com and hence might be missing incidents, specially from regional local media.
  2. We are populating our database only from the first report of the incident. To maintain consistency of the database across all cases, we are not looking at follow up stories for any of these incidents. Information from first reports are not verified and finer details are not yet known.
  3. There are 4 fields where we have taken an approximation.
  • Did the lynching take place in an urban or rural area?
    • Approach: Filled up approximately after a quick Google search of the exact village or town name. Ideally, we should have used RBI's classification of the areas by population.
  • Was the lynching planned? We followed the following approach:
    • Mark it as "Planned" if it was clearly planned against a specific victim. Example - a village meeting that decides that someone needs to be lynched.
    • Mark it as "Neighbourhood watch" where local citizens were on the lookout for wrongdoers and prepared to punish him/her/them. Example - Sometimes police ask villagers to stay on watch themselves against child lifters.
    • Mark it as "Spontaneous" if the accused lynchers found the victims out of coincidence.
    • Mark it as "Don't know" if nothing is mentioned in the article.
  • Religion of the victim and the accused.
    • Approach: We are taking the religion directly from the article if mentioned. If not mentioned, then we are deducing from name where it is possible to ascertain with some surety. In case of confusion or if victims are from multiple religions then we are leaving it blank.
  • If the allegation on the victim were true, would it be a punishable offence in that state?
    • The objective of this field is not to justify any illegal act or lynching.
    • The definition of “lynching” is the beating or killing of a suspected criminal. This field is being collected to analyse if the mob is even accusing the victim of an illegality.
    • The legality or illegality of the victim's actions does not justify the lynching.

What should I do if I have more information on a mob justice incident that is not recorded, or find an error?

We welcome all contributions of information that may improve the quality of our data. #TheLynched is a comprehensive and ongoing process of verification, as we continue to work from an inaccurate standard toward a more perfect standard. Please pass on your information as soon as possible to rparrikh@icfj.news.
